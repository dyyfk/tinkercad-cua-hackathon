# [1DAY_1CAD] BEAR DOLL (Tinkercad : Know-how / Style / Education)

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fs2h1acNG38
Author: 1DAY_1CAD : Tinkercad 3D Design

This build follows the tutorial as a Tinkercad-style primitive assembly: large rounded masses first, mirrored appendages second, face landmarks third, and flat title text last.

## Tutorial Stages

### Step 1: Reference silhouette (00:00)

Primitive: assembled primitives

The finished model is a seated teddy-bear doll with a round head, oval body, protruding muzzle, side ears, small black eyes, arms, feet, and BEAR DOLL text.

### Step 2: Head and body (01:00)

Primitive: sphere/ellipsoid

Block the main silhouette with large rounded spheres before adding details.

### Step 3: Muzzle and face base (02:00)

Primitive: flattened sphere

Add a shallow oval muzzle on the front of the head, then keep later eye and nose landmarks aligned to it.

### Step 4: Eyes, nose, ears (04:30)

Primitive: small spheres/ellipsoids

Mirror small black eyes and a nose across the centerline; add round side ears with lighter inner pads.

### Step 5: Body assembly (06:00)

Primitive: ellipsoid body plus belly patch

Place the body under the head with a pale oval belly patch, preserving the plush doll proportions.

### Step 6: Arms (08:30)

Primitive: rotated ellipsoid

Use mirrored tapered oval arms that hang down the sides of the body.

### Step 7: Legs and feet (10:00)

Primitive: rotated ellipsoid

Use forward-facing oval feet and small sole pads so the doll reads as seated.

### Step 8: Final title (12:00)

Primitive: extruded block text

Add BEAR DOLL text as flat yellow geometry on the workplane next to the doll.
